Jinghan Yang

I am a final year PhD in computer science and computer engineering at Washington University at St. Louis, where I adverised by Yevgeniy Vorobeychik. My research interests fall broadly within the fields of adversarial machine learning, deep learning, and reinforcement learning. Throughout my Ph.D. I have explored the vulnerabilities of deep learning models in the context of computer vision as well as autonomous driving. I am particularly interested in the development of deep learning models for autonomous driving, and my recent work has focused on designing self-diving systems which are robust to both adversarial perturbations (e.g., malicious graffiti painted on road signs) as well as natural changes in the driving environment (e.g., weather, construction, or accidents). Recently I have also been working on robust algorithms for multi-agent deep reinforcement learning.
